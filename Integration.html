Understanding the Integration of Self

Who I am is a complete picture made by putting together different parts of myself: my Physical Self, my Sexual Self, my Philosophical beliefs, how I see myself in society (Socio-anthropological), and my Psychological growth. These parts aren't separate, they all connect and shape who I am, what I believe, and how I act.

My Physical Self is about my body, how I see it (Body Image), how it looks (Body Appearance), how fit it is (Body Fitness), and my daily Health and Lifestyle choices. How I feel about my body affects my confidence and how I do things, including my relationships. My Sexual Self is about understanding myself sexually and my gender (SOGIESC), how I like to be in relationships (power, boundaries), what I value in love, my relationship types, consent, and sexual health. This is key to how I connect with others and express myself intimately. These physical and sexual parts are not separate from the rest of me; they are part of the whole. My philosophical beliefs (like Socrates' "Know Thyself") give me my core values and help me understand my purpose. These beliefs also guide my physical and sexual choices, making sure they match what I truly believe. How I see myself in society (Socio-anthropological), like Cooley's "Looking Glass Self," shows me that other people influence who I am. This affects how I view my body, gender, and sexual relationships, and how I fit into the world. My Psychological growth, as explained by Erikson, means I've learned and changed through different life stages. This includes growing in how I feel about my body, figuring out my sexual identity, and building a strong sense of self through experiences.

How These Selves Work Together

Integrating all parts of myself, Physical, Sexual, Philosophical, Socio-anthropological, and Psychological, creates a strong balance in my life. When I make choices or deal with problems, I use all of them.

My philosophical side helps me find what feels real and true to my values. This means my choices about my Physical Self (like body image and fitness) and my Sexual Self (like consent and relationship values) are guided by my core beliefs. My socio-anthropological side helps me see how my choices affect my relationships and place in society. This includes how my physical appearance or sexual identity might be perceived, and how I navigate social expectations. My psychological side helps me understand my emotions and growth, which is essential for managing insecurities related to my Physical Self and for developing healthy Sexual Self actions like communication and self-acceptance. My physical side helps me shape habits around body image, fitness, and lifestyle choices, which in turn can affect my confidence in social and sexual situations. My sexual side helps me keep healthy communication, consent, and relationship values, which are crucial for my overall psychological well-being and social interactions.

For example, when I decided to step away from trying to fit into the "cool" group in elementary school, it wasn't just a social decision. It was a philosophical choice to honor who I truly am (Know Thyself), a recognition that I don't need others' validation to define myself (Looking Glass Self), and an understanding of my psychological need for originality and self-acceptance. This decision also impacted my physical self by freeing me from the pressure to conform to certain appearances or activities, and it laid the foundation for developing healthier sexual and relational values based on authenticity rather than others' approval.
