Understanding the Integration of Self

Who I am is a complete picture made by bringing together different parts of myself: my Physical Self, my Sexual Self, my Philosophical beliefs, how I see myself in society (Socio-anthropological), my Psychological growth, my Material Self, my Spiritual Self, my Political Self, and my Digital Self. These parts are not separate, they all connect and shape who I am, what I believe, and how I act.

My Physical Self is about my body, how I see it (Body Image), how it looks (Body Appearance), how fit it is (Body Fitness), and my daily Health and Lifestyle choices. How I feel about my body affects my confidence and how I do things, including my relationships. My Sexual Self is about understanding myself sexually and my gender (SOGIESC), how I like to be in relationships (power, boundaries), what I value in love, my relationship types, consent, and sexual health. This is important for how I connect with others and express myself intimately. These physical and sexual parts are not separate from the rest of me; they are part of the whole.

My Material Self looks at my relationship with things I own, how I manage resources, and how I feel about having enough money and using things wisely. My Spiritual Self explores my inner values, my sense of right and wrong, my beliefs, and my connection to something bigger than myself, focusing on growing as a person and acting ethically. My Political Self includes my understanding of how the government works, my awareness of community issues, and how I participate in politics and societal matters. My Digital Self shows my online identity, my digital footprint, and how I act and present myself online.

My philosophical beliefs (like Socrates' idea to 'Know Thyself') give me my main values and help me understand my purpose. These beliefs also guide my choices about my body, sex life, possessions, spirituality, politics, and online life, making sure they match what I truly believe. How I see myself in society, like Cooley's 'Looking Glass Self' idea, shows me that other people's views influence who I am. This affects how I see my body, gender, relationships, material needs, spiritual path, political views, and online presence, and how I fit into the world. My Psychological growth, as explained by Erikson, means I have learned and changed through different stages of life. This includes growing in how I feel about my body, figuring out my sexual identity, developing my material security, deepening my spiritual understanding, forming my political views, managing my online identity, and building a strong sense of self through experiences.

All these parts Physical, Sexual, Philosophical, Socio-anthropological, Psychological, Material, Spiritual, Political, and Digital work together to create the full and always changing picture of who I am.

How These Selves Work Together

Bringing together all parts of myself, Physical, Sexual, Philosophical, Socio-anthropological, Psychological, Material, Spiritual, Political, and Digital, creates a strong balance in my life. When I make choices or deal with problems, I use all of them.

My philosophical side helps me find what feels real and true to my values. This means my choices about my Physical Self (like how I see my body and my fitness) and my Sexual Self (like consent and what I value in relationships) are guided by my core beliefs. My socio-anthropological side helps me see how my choices affect my relationships and my place in society. This includes how my physical appearance or sexual identity might be seen by others, and how I fit into social expectations. My psychological side helps me understand my feelings and growth, which is important for handling worries about my Physical Self and for developing healthy actions in my Sexual Self, such as good communication and accepting myself. My physical side helps me build habits around body image, fitness, and lifestyle choices, which can then affect my confidence in social and sexual situations. My sexual side helps me maintain good communication, consent, and relationship values, which are vital for my overall psychological well-being and social interactions.

My Material Self affects how I manage my belongings and resources, which can impact my psychological well-being by providing a sense of security or causing worry. My spiritual beliefs guide my values and purpose, influencing my decisions about my physical health, relationships, and how I interact with the world, including my political views and online behavior. My political awareness shapes my understanding of society and my role in it, affecting my actions both online and offline, and guiding how I use my material resources for community involvement. My digital self is where I express and explore many of these other selves, and my choices online can reflect or even shape my physical habits, material desires, spiritual journey, political engagement, and social interactions.

For example, when I decided to stop trying to fit in with the "cool" group in elementary school, it was more than just a social decision. It was a philosophical choice to honor who I truly am (Know Thyself), a realization that I do not need others' approval to define myself (Looking Glass Self), and an understanding of my psychological need for uniqueness and self-acceptance. This decision also affected my physical self by removing the pressure to match certain looks or activities, and it set the stage for developing healthier sexual and relationship values based on being genuine rather than seeking others' approval. This choice also influenced my material self by reducing the pressure to buy certain things to fit in, my spiritual self by strengthening my inner moral compass, my political self by encouraging independent thinking, and my digital self by promoting honest online expression.
