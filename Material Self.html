Material Self

This section explores my relationship with material possessions, my approach to managing resources, and my perception of financial well-being and responsible consumption.

Based on our previous topic about the Material Self, my material and consumer self is about how the things I own and buy shape my identity, and how my own desires and the world around me influence my choices. It's not just about having stuff, but how it becomes part of who I am and how I decide what to get. My needs, wants, and motivations are the drivers for what I buy. For example, I might need study aids or want the latest tech I see online, and I'm often motivated by fitting in with friends, enjoying my hobbies, or rewarding myself. Society and my environment also heavily influence me, as I see what my friends have, what influencers promote on social media, and what trends are popular in my college setting, all of which shape what I desire and ultimately purchase.
